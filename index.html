Honest status: AccInt is pre-product. But it is not a mockup — the system runs its builder's own operation today: it finds the right people, writes the first lines, sends from his real accounts, verifies every send on the live page, and compounds what worked into memory. Its first customer is the person who built it. Early access opens in small batches.

how it works

You set the goal.
It does the work.

1

Say it in plain words

"Find me 20 leads like our last three customers and start conversations." No prompts to engineer, no workflows to build — the goal is the interface.

2

It uses every tool a person would

A real browser in your LinkedIn, email, CRM when the work lives there — no integrations to wait on. Code and scripts when that's faster. Documents, spreadsheets, APIs when they fit. The browser is one of its hands, not the whole product. Anything that leaves your machine waits for your OK first.

3

It checks reality, then remembers

A send isn't done until it's confirmed on the live page. Replies, results, and failures flow back into one memory — so next week's work starts smarter than this week's.

RECEIPTS, NOT VIBES

Every claim of "done" needs evidence from the world. The system's own opinion of itself is deliberately distrusted.

YOUR CONSENT IS STRUCTURAL

Sending, posting, paying, deleting — hard-gated behind your word. Not a setting. A law in the kernel.

YOUR DATA STAYS YOURS

One local memory on your machine. No training on your accounts, no data leaving for someone else's model.
